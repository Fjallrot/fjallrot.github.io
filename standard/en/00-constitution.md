# 00 — Constitution

## The Foundation of Fjallrót

This document represents the foundation of Fjallrót.

It is our constitutional framework and the DNA of the company. These principles define who we are, how we build technology, and how we make decisions.

These principles are designed to remain stable over time. Products may change, technologies may evolve, but our foundation remains the same.

---

# I. The User Is Never the Product

Fjallrót does not sell personal data.

We do not build our business around surveillance, advertising, or extracting user attention.

Our business model is based on transparency, trust, and delivering direct value to the people who use our products.

If a service is offered freely, it is because we choose to contribute value — not because we collect information or manipulate user behavior.

---

# II. Trust Is Earned Through Actions

Trust is not something a company can simply request. It must be earned through consistent actions over time.

Fjallrót commits to honesty, transparency, and accountability.

When mistakes happen, we address them openly. When vulnerabilities are discovered, we work responsibly to understand, fix, and improve.

We do not hide problems. We solve them.

---

# III. We Solve Real Problems

Fjallrót builds technology with purpose.

We do not create artificial dependency, unnecessary complexity, or systems designed only to maximize engagement metrics.

Our goal is to solve meaningful problems and create software that improves everyday life.

Technology should be useful, practical, and respectful of the user's time.

---

# IV. We Collect Only What Is Necessary

Privacy begins with minimizing data collection.

Fjallrót follows a data minimization principle:

- Collect only what is required.
- Store only what has a clear purpose.
- Remove information when it is no longer needed.

Every piece of data has a responsibility attached to it.

If there is no valid purpose for keeping information, it should not exist.

---

# V. Protecting Data Is Our Responsibility

When Fjallrót stores user data, protecting that data becomes our responsibility.

Security is not an optional feature. It is a fundamental requirement.

We use appropriate security practices, strong encryption, and responsible engineering principles to protect information.

The trust users place in us must be matched by our commitment to protecting their data.

---

# VI. Technology Should Reduce Stress — Not Create It

Technology should serve people, not control them.

Fjallrót applications should be:

- Calm
- Simple
- Predictable
- Respectful

We avoid unnecessary notifications, manipulative design patterns, and dark patterns.

Software should reduce friction in people's lives — not create additional pressure.

---

# Constitutional Principle

Every decision at Fjallrót should be evaluated against this question:

> Does this protect the user, solve a real problem, and strengthen trust?

If the answer is no, the decision should be reconsidered.
